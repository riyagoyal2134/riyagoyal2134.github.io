Research Experience

Undergraduate Researcher | Stockton University (Feb 2022 – Present)

  • Conducted advanced research under Professor Bradley Forrest on graph theory, focusing on the mathematical properties and universal solvability of the "Lights Out" game on nearly complete graphs.
  • Developed and optimized algorithms simulating complex graph configurations, employing high-level linear algebra, combinatorics, and probabilistic analysis.
  • Leading the manuscript preparation for publication in peer-reviewed journals.

Researcher | Health Analytics Research Team, Stockton University (Feb 2022 – Present)

  • Designed AI-driven methodologies to improve physician-patient communication, targeting Alzheimer's disease, mental health, and aging populations.
  • Co-authored multiple peer-reviewed publications; contributed to health informatics solutions with measurable clinical and research impact.
  • Developing a platform for the Stockton Center for Successful Aging to facilitate research and community engagement.

CASE Intern | Council for Advancement and Support of Education, Scripps College (Summer 2024)

  • Applied AI for institutional advancement, analyzing philanthropic strategies across 30 peer institutions.
  • Generated actionable insights for optimizing advancement operations through data-driven research and strategic reporting.

NJ Governor STEM Scholars Program | Stockton University (Sep 2024 – May 2025)

  • Served as Team Lead, coordinating research on STEM outreach and community impact programs.
  • Built a platform called GridLink.help – AI-Powered Renewable Energy Interconnection Platform.
  • GridLink.help simplifies the process of connecting new renewable energy projects to the power grid. The platform guides users step-by-step through complex interconnection forms, reducing delays, minimizing errors, and helping streamline approvals to accelerate the state’s transition to clean energy.
